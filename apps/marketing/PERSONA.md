# Primary learner persona

This site is currently run as a single institute's own course platform (not a
multi-tenant SaaS pitch to other institutes — see the memory note on current
usage mode). Its actual audience is the student deciding whether to enroll,
so site copy should be written for them, not for a prospective institute
owner. One clear persona, not a spread of loosely-related ones — that's what
keeps the homepage, curriculum framing, and CTAs coherent.

## Aarav Mehta, 19 — Tier-2 city, exam-prep student

**Situation:** Finished Class 12, preparing for a competitive/professional
entrance exam. Local coaching is expensive or a long commute away; moving to
a metro city for it isn't an option his family can support.

**Goals**
- Pass the exam with a real understanding of the material, not just rote videos.
- Get his doubts answered in real time, not just watch a static recording.
- Study on his own schedule — evenings, between other commitments.

**Pain points**
- YouTube content is free but unstructured and not exam-focused.
- Paid courses are a leap of faith — no way to judge quality before paying.
- Shared/mobile-only internet access; a laptop isn't guaranteed.
- Has been burned before by a course that turned out shallow or disorganized.

**Device & behavior**
- Primarily a phone; sometimes a shared family laptop in the evening.
- Price-sensitive — compares a few options before committing.
- Trusts a clear, upfront syllabus more than testimonials or hype.
- Wants to sample before buying.

**What actually gets him to enroll**
1. A visible, module-by-module curriculum before he pays — not a vague description.
2. A free preview lesson so he can judge teaching quality directly.
3. A live class option for doubt-clearing, with recordings as backup if he misses it.
4. One clear price, one clear access period — no hidden tiers or fine print.
5. It works on his phone.

## What this changes on the site

The product already matches this persona well (free previews, module-wise
curriculum on every course page, live+recorded combo, flat pricing, mobile
web). What didn't match was the *copy*: the homepage, header CTA, about page,
and contact page were still written to recruit institute owners onto a SaaS
platform ("Start your institute — free"). That's the wrong audience for a
single-institute site. Rewritten to speak to Aarav directly: lead with what
he gets as a learner, route the primary CTA to the course catalog instead of
an institute-signup flow, and keep the "prove it before you pay" angle
front and center on both the homepage and each course page.
